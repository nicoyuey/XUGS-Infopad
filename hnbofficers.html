Classroom Officers

The elected class officers of the class assist the teacher in class management and government.

You should extend to them due respect and cooperation. They have the authority granted by the teacher and are to be obeyed courteously and promptly when acting within the authority they have received.

Class President

  • is in charge of the class when there is no teacher
  • represents the class in the Pupil Councils
  • helps the teacher in organizing the class for some class activities
  • reminds the other class officers of their duties

Class Secretary

  • does some writing for the teacher
  • helps the teacher in keeping record of schedule of activities

Class Treasurer

  • helps the teacher collect approved funds
  • keeps a record of payees
  • keeps record of amount collected and amount spent
  • keeps the collected funds in a safe place

Class Beadle

  • takes down names of pupils who are absent from or late in class
  • keeps daily record of the attendance
  • sees to it that the lights and electric fans are turned off when they are not needed

Class Marshall

  • helps the teacher maintain order in class
  • keeps a record of infractions of classroom rules
  • helps the teacher report to the Prefect of Students, grave violations of, or repeated violations of classroom rules
